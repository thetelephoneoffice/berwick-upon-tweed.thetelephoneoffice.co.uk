Berwick Telephone Exchange: A Historical Overview

History

The first telephone exchange in Berwick-Upon-Tweed was opened in 1894 by the National Telephone Company in the attic of No 5 Hide Hill. Twelve years later, a new post office was built in Woolmarket with space for a telephone exchange. When the General Post Office (GPO) took over the company system in 1912, the exchange was moved there.

Modern Infrastructure

Today, the Berwick Exchange serves approximately 6,430 residential premises and 542 non-residential premises. It provides ADSL broadband services and has been enabled for ADSL Max since 2006. The exchange also offers FTTC and FTTP services in some areas.

Future of Telecommunication in Berwick

With the advent of Voice over Internet Protocol (VoIP) technology, the future of telecommunication in Berwick looks promising. VoIP allows for more efficient and cost-effective communication, making it an attractive option for both residential and commercial users.
